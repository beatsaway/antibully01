Understanding Bullying: What It Is and How It Hurts

Learning about words that hurt and how to be kind

What is Bullying?

When we call someone names like "kebab" or "cookie" or make fun of how they look (like their nails or hair), it might seem like just joking around. But these words can really hurt people's feelings. This page will help you understand why these words hurt and what happens to both the person being bullied and the person doing the bullying.

Did You Know?

Scientists who study how kids get along have found that words can hurt just as much as hitting someone. Our brains react to mean words the same way they react to physical pain!

Different Types of Bullying

There are several kinds of bullying that use words. Look at these examples:

Type of Bullying What It Is Examples
Name-calling Using hurtful names to call someone Calling someone "kebab" or "cookie" over and over again
Appearance Bullying Making fun of how someone looks Making jokes about someone's "long nails" or saying they have "funny hair"
Cultural Bullying Making fun of someone's background or culture Using food names (like "kebab") that make fun of where someone's family is from
Repeated "Jokes" Saying something is "just a joke" when it actually hurts feelings Making the same comment about someone's appearance day after day, even if they don't laugh

Example: When Is It Bullying?

If you call someone "cookie" once because you both think it's funny, and they laugh too, that might be a friendly nickname. But if you keep calling them "cookie" every day when they answer questions, especially if they look sad or ask you to stop, that's bullying.

How Bullying Hurts People

When someone is bullied with words or comments about how they look, many things can happen:

Body Feelings

  • Getting headaches or stomachaches before school
  • Having trouble sleeping
  • Feeling tired all the time

Mind and Feelings

  • Feeling sad or worried a lot
  • Not liking how they look
  • Feeling bad about themselves

Friends and Others

  • Not wanting to be around other people
  • Having trouble making new friends
  • Being afraid to speak up in class

School Work

  • Having trouble paying attention in class
  • Not wanting to come to school
  • Getting lower grades

Long-Term Effects

People who were bullied as kids can sometimes still feel sad or worried about it even when they're grown-ups! The words can stick in their minds for many years.

What Happens to People Who Bully Others?

Did you know that bullying can also cause problems for the person doing the bullying? Here's what can happen:

Growing-Up Problems

  • Having trouble with their own feelings later in life
  • Finding it hard to make good choices
  • Feeling bad about themselves as they get older

Friendship Problems

  • Having a harder time making real friends
  • Getting in trouble at school or work
  • People might not trust them

Real Story

Did you know there were some students who were accepted but then rejected by universities? When the school found out they had been mean to others in the past, they weren't allowed to attend anymore! What we say and do can follow us for a long time.
